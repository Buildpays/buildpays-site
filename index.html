The payroll pillar

The agreement is 186 pages. Your payroll shouldn't need all of them open.

A CFMEU Victoria construction EBA sets flat double-time overtime, weekend minimum payments, RDO accruals on a 36-hour week, per-project site allowances, daily fares and travel, casual loadings and inclement weather banks. Getting one of them wrong in a spreadsheet means back-pay, disputes, and audits. BuildPays encodes the rules once, so every timesheet comes out right.

FAQ

Questions subcontractors ask us.

Is there a free trial?

No self-serve trial. Payroll deserves a proper start: book a demo with your enterprise agreement and a recent pay run. If BuildPays fits, we set your company up with a free pilot period — running alongside your existing process until the MYOB export matches it to the cent. You go live when the numbers prove it, not before.

Does it replace MYOB?

No — it feeds it. MYOB stays your payroll system of record and holds every pay rate. We handle everything before the pay run: rostering, EBA-classified timesheets, approvals, and a units-only import file matched to your payroll categories and job codes.

Which enterprise agreements do you support?

The engine is built on the CFMEU Victoria construction & general pattern used across formwork, structure and civil subcontractor agreements approved by the Fair Work Commission. During setup we configure it to your specific agreement — classifications, allowances and site allowance tiers.

How do workers use it?

It's a web app they add to their phone's home screen — no app store, no passwords to forget. They see their roster, submit a timesheet in under 30 seconds, and get an SMS when tomorrow's site is confirmed.

What about site inductions and high risk work licences?

Both are first-class records. The roster physically blocks allocating a worker to a site they aren't inducted for, and tickets are validated against WorkSafe Victoria validity periods — high risk work licences renew every five years, first aid every three — with expiry warnings before they bite.

Can we get just the dockets, without the payroll system?

Yes — the platform is modular. Take dayworks dockets and cost reports on their own at a flat monthly fee, add rostering or onboarding as you need them, or run the full system billed per employee paid. Timesheets and approvals are included in every plan, and dockets built from timesheets carry verified hours — a cross-check that docket-only plans can add any time by upgrading.

How are public holidays and RDOs handled?

By date, not by worker choice. The app holds the gazetted Victorian public holiday calendar and your industry RDO calendar; any hours submitted on those dates classify at penalty rates automatically, and employees who correctly don't work them have their entitlement lines generated at export time — public holiday pay to the right job code, RDOs taken with fares and site allowance to the project where they accrued.

How do dayworks dockets and client charging work?

Each project carries its own rate schedule — all-inclusive normal-time and overtime rates, or base rates with separate travel and site allowance lines. Workers or nominated leading hands raise dockets with hours computed from times (never typed), the client supervisor countersigns on-screen or via a secure emailed link, and admins export an Excel cost report per project and date range. Optionally, a signature from a verified client email domain can auto-approve the matching timesheets on day-labour jobs.

Is our data safe?

Each company is an isolated tenant with role-based access: workers see only themselves, site managers see their crews, and only your admins see the company. You can export your data at any time.
